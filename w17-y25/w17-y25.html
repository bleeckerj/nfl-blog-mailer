In 1979 and 1981 Byte magazine, which was beautifully and nerdily futuristic, published issues featuring AI. What was that, then? And what would it have become had the home brew computer club folks somehow found a way through some contrivance and twist of fate, to conjure a kind of AI that took hold of the world?

I found an old (actually two old) issues of Byte magazine that happened to have as their cover story "Artificial Intelligence".

Now, that might not be so weird given everything that's going on. But it may be if I told you that the issues were from 1979 and 1981. And it starts one wondering: what kind of world would we be living in today if the AI from 45 years ago somehow, through some twist of technological counter-historical contingency, became ‚Äòa thing‚Äô.

In such a scenario, we would expect to see AI deeply intertwined with the aesthetics, cultural values, and technological aspects of that era, leading to a (possibly) very different trajectory in how machines interact with humans and shape society.¬†
